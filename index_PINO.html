Monte Carlo (MC)

Simulates thousands of paths to average the payoff.

  • High Accuracy
  • Extremely Slow
  • Impractical for frequent calibration

Fast Fourier Transform

Inverts characteristic function numerically.

  • Faster than MC

PINO Approach

  • Differentiable (Greeks)

Adaptive PINO Framework

Combining Spectral Convolutions, Hard Physics Constraints, and Adversarial Loss Balancing to solve the Feynman-Kac PDE.

Unlike standard PINNs that sample random points, PINO learns the physics across the entire domain simultaneously. A structured mesh is created across Stock Price (S), Variance (V), and Time (t). The model minimizes the PDE residual at every point on this grid at once.

Addressing the Gibbs Phenomenon

At maturity (t=T), the option price has a sharp "kink" (non-differentiable point). Standard spectral methods oscillate wildly here. We solve this by decomposing the solution:

V(S,V,t) = Payoff(S) + TimeValue(S,V,t) √ó (1 - t/T)
  • Payoff(S): Analytically known, handles the sharp kink.
  • TimeValue: Smooth function, easily learned by the neural network.
  • Result: Terminal boundary condition is structurally satisfied. No explicit boundary loss needed.

Adversarial Weight Tuning

Manual tuning of loss weights (e.g., valuing PDE error vs. Greek error) is difficult. WamOL automates this via a Minimax Game.

The Conflict

1. Network tries to minimize total loss.
2. WamOL tries to maximize total loss by increasing the weight of the hardest component.

Outcome: The network is forced to focus on the "weakest link" at any stage of training, ensuring uniform accuracy across PDE, Boundary, and Greek constraints.
